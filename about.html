Contents:

Commemoration of a Roman Public Official, Aquileia.


Summary of Support:

Statue base made of marble with profile-framed inscription field.


Height: 100.5 cm

Width: 64 cm

Depth: 22.5 cm


Condition: Complete epigraph.


Layout: There is one column and 20 lines.


Letter style: The letters decrease in size down the rows of text.

Letter height: 4.5 - 2 cm


Place Created: Aquileia
Date: 122 CE – 150 CE
Place Found: Aquileia
Currently housed: Aquileia, Museo Archeologico, Lapidario.

Commentary:

"For these I set no limits, world or time, But make a gift of empire without end."--Virgil, the Aenied


This base of a statue dedicated to A.P. Nepos Aponius Italicus Manilianus C. Licinius Pollio, an official under Trajan and Hadrian. According to Werner Eck, he probably came from the Iberian peninsula and obtained the support of the Emperor Trajan for most of his career.5 The statue itself was unearthed in Aquileia 1805, taken to Vienna in 1806, and returned to the Museo Archeologico in 1978.6


What makes this object particularly interesting is the number of places mentioned (no less than 8 geographic entities), providing a glimpse into the travels a Roman functionary made during the course of his career. During his life A.P. Nepos was stationed in Britain, Germany, Macedonia and Thrace, and montiored several main roads throughout Italy. This epigraph reveals the extent of the Roman Empire by the second century CE., and serves as a reminder of Jupiter's prophcecy from Aenid.


Arthur Gordon suggestes that we read his titles in descending order7, so that we can trace a carerer starting as a member of the triumvir capitalis, then overseer of the viae Cassiae, Clodiae, Ciminiae novae, Traianae; legate of several legions, before wraping up his career as counsul of Britain, where he helped to construct Trajan's wall.8


This particular inscription is well documented with several translations available. I have attempted to render it fairly literally with an ear to modern sensibilites.
